Risk Radar

Early-access cannibalization (no net incremental demand)

Trigger: Loyal customers pull planned holiday purchases into the early-access window, reducing later-period peak sales and compressing overall revenue without increasing total spend.

Detection: Instrument weekly footfall, per-visit AOV, cohorted repeat-rate, and tagged transactions (early-access vs public). Measure net incremental sales vs a holdout baseline across the 8-week test window and monitor uplift decay into flagship weekends.

Mitigation: Run randomized holdouts to measure true incrementality; limit early-access eligibility by SKU, spend threshold or service (exclusive lines, early shipping) rather than across-the-board discounts; shorten early window if cannibalization shows; reserve the highest-margin SKUs and flagship discounts for later phases.

Mitigation: Use smaller public discounts early and reserve deeper price cuts for targeted segments; complement price with non-price incentives (exclusive products, bundles, fulfillment perks); enforce inventory controls and SKU gating; pivot cadence quickly if markdown elasticity shows outsized margin loss.
